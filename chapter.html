DFHS Technology Student Association Chapter

Who Are We?

The Dutch Fork High School Technology Student Association is a student-based competitive technology program. Our program has over 30 students actively competing at the TSA Venue, while the entire school has membership in the program. Dutch Fork High School competes both on the state and the national level.

Creed

"I believe that Technology Education holds an important place in my life in the technical world. I believe there is a need for the development of good attitudes concerning work, tools, materials, experimentation, and processes of industry. Guided by my teachers, artisans from industry, and my own initiative, I will strive to do my best in making my school, community, state, and nation better places in which to live. I will accept the responsibilities that are mine. I will accept the theories that are supported by proper evidence. I will explore on my own for safer, more effective methods of working and living. I will strive to develop a cooperative attitude and will exercise tact and respect for other individuals. Through the work of my hands and mind, I will express my ideas to the best of my ability. I will make it my goal to do better each day the task before me, and to be steadfast in my belief in my God, and my fellow Americans."

Motto

"Learning to Lead in a Technical World."

Community Service and Chapter Activities

What Have We Done as a Chapter?

As our own school chapter, we work throughout the year on activities such as team building, portfolio developing, and simply having fun.

Throughout our history, we have performed many feats relating to community service. We volunteer at Leaphart Elementary and teach younger students engineering and technology-related skills. Additionally, the Dutch Fork High School TSA chapter mentors middle school students and works with other high school teachers in order to build classroom-related projects. We have helped with the Special Olympics, worked with ROTC for a mud run charity, and volunteered at several events such as the SCARAB board gaming convention, among several other community service projects.

Competitions

Dutch Fork High School competes yearly both on the state and the national level. Most years, students compete among a vast range of events and explore new themes and topics during their competitions. Each year, we have had students place on the state level and several have furthermore competed and placed on the national stage.
